## Proposed Pricing

| Tier | Price | Content | Delivery |
|------|-------|---------|----------|
| **Free** | €0 | Top 3 opportunities + sector trends | Weekly email |
| **Paid** | €19/mo or €199/yr | Full ranked list (10–15), scoring breakdown, risk analysis, company profiles | Weekly email + web archive |
| **Premium** | €49/mo or €499/yr | Everything in Paid + custom alerts by sector/criteria, real-time notification, priority support, API access | Real-time + weekly |

**Paid rationale:** €19/mo is the "impulse buy" tier for professionals. At €199/yr, it's a business expense. Comparable to a professional news subscription.

**Annual discount:** ~15% to encourage annual lock-in.

## Key Pain Point: Creditor Double-Payment Liability

Under German law, when a business partner enters insolvency proceedings, any subsequent invoice payments must be directed to the designated liquidator — not the company itself. If a creditor mistakenly pays the bankrupt entity directly, they are **not discharged of their debt** and can be forced to pay the liquidator a second time.

**This is a sharp, specific, costly problem that real-time insolvency alerts solve.**

Real-world scenario:
1. Creditor sends invoice to a client on standard 30-day terms
2. Client files for insolvency on day 15 — creditor is unaware
3. Creditor receives payment from client on day 35 and marks it as settled
4. Liquidator discovers the payment went to the bankrupt entity, not the official account
5. Creditor must pay the liquidator again — effectively paying twice

The newsletter / alert service provides an early warning system that prevents this.

## Unit Economics (Estimate)

**Costs per month (MVP):**
- beehiiv: €0 for free tier / $0 SaaS fee (0% revenue cut)
- Insolvenz-Radar API: €29–49/mo (once automated)
- Total: ~€30–50/mo fixed

**Revenue target:** €380/mo (breakeven at ~20 paid subscribers at €19/mo)

**LTV estimate:** If avg retention is 12 months at €19/mo → €228/paid subscriber
**CAC estimate:** €0–20 per subscriber (organic channels)
**LTV/CAC:** 11x+ — excellent unit economics

**Note on beehiiv vs Substack economics:**
- Substack takes 10% of subscription revenue (cuts into margin significantly)
- beehiiv takes 0% — flat SaaS pricing only
- At 50 paid subscribers × €19/mo: Substack takes €95/mo vs beehiiv takes €0
