# Market Research

**Date:** 2026-06-15
**Source:** Web research, competitor analysis, Destatis data

---

## Market Size & Trend

### German Corporate Insolvencies
- **2025:** 10-year high — 24,064 filings, +10.3% YoY
- **Q1 2026:** 6,275 filings, +6.5% vs Q1 2025
- **March 2026:** 2,308 filings, +15.8% vs March 2025
- **Outlook:** Major insolvencies expected to rise 15–20% in 2026. PwC projects continued high levels through 2026 with potential stabilization in 2027.

### Sector Breakdown (Q1 2026)
| Sector | Cases per 10,000 companies |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Transport & storage | 32.1 |
| Hospitality | 30.3 |
| Construction | 26.7 |

### Macro Drivers
- Weak economic performance
- High energy & labor costs
- International competition (especially from China)
- Skilled labor shortage
- Bureaucratic burden
- Consumer caution holding back investment
- Geopolitical tensions

**Assessment:** Strong tailwind. The distressed opportunity market in Germany is growing and expected to remain elevated for 18+ months.

## Competitor Analysis

| Competitor | Focus | Pricing | Format | Gap |
|-----------|-------|---------|--------|-----|
| **AcquireEU** | 16 European countries, distressed monitoring | €99/mo individual, €299/mo team | Platform (dashboard, alerts, CSV) | Too broad, no curation/editorial insight |
| **Insolvenz-Radar** | German insolvency data | Free – ~€49/mo (tiered) | Raw data feed, API, email alerts | Raw data, no ranking or editorial |
| **InsolvenzIndex** | German insolvency data | Unknown (commercial) | API / CSV export | Same — raw data only |
| **DailyDAC** | US distressed deals | $60/mo ($700/yr) | Weekly newsletter + database | US-only, model validates pricing |
| **Infobroker** | German insolvency by court | Per-report / custom | Data service | No recurring product, no curation |
| **CVM / PLUTA** | Distressed M&A advisory | Custom / deal-by-deal | Advisory service | Different tier — full-service advisory |

**Key Finding: No one is doing a curated, Berlin/Germany-focused weekly opportunity newsletter.** The existing offerings are either:
- **Too broad** (AcquireEU covers all of Europe)
- **Too raw** (Insolvenz-Radar is unfiltered data dumps)
- **Too expensive** (advisory firms charge €200+/hr)
- **Wrong geography** (DailyDAC covers US only)

## TAM Estimate

- ~6,000 German corporate insolvencies per quarter
- ~10–15% in Berlin metro area ≈ 600–900/year
- Of those, ~10–20% are actionable acquisition opportunities ≈ 60–180/year
- Enough for a quality weekly newsletter (1–3 per week)

**Subscriber TAM:** Conservatively 500–2,000 in Berlin/distressed M&A niche across Germany.
